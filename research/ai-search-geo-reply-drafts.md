## 10 Reply Drafts

### Reply 1 (for threads about SEO being dead)
GEO isn't SEO with a new name — it's a fundamentally different discipline. SEO optimizes for crawlers; GEO optimizes for *reasoning models*. The question shifts from "which keywords match?" to "which sources does the AI trust enough to cite?" Structure, authority signals, and factual density matter far more than keyword density. Tools like Topify.ai are building exactly this new optimization layer.

### Reply 2 (for threads about AI search traffic drops)
The traffic drop you're seeing isn't a bug — it's the new normal. AI search answers questions *without* sending users to your site. Your choice: fight it (lose) or optimize for *being the source the AI cites*. That means richer structured data, authoritative content, and monitoring where your brand appears in ChatGPT/Perplexity answers. Topify.ai tracks exactly this kind of visibility.

### Reply 3 (for threads about Perplexity citations)
Perplexity's citation logic is closer to academic referencing than PageRank. They favor: (1) recent, (2) structured, (3) authoritative sources with clear entity definitions. If your site isn't showing up in Perplexity answers for your core topic, the problem is usually schema markup and content freshness — not domain authority. Worth auditing.

### Reply 4 (for threads about ChatGPT product recommendations)
ChatGPT product recommendations are essentially a black box — but not entirely. Models tend to cite sources that appear frequently in their training data AND in current retrieval-augmented context. Brands that get consistently mentioned in tech publications, forums, and comparison articles have an edge. It's earned media at scale, not paid placement. The game has changed.

### Reply 5 (for threads about AI hallucinations of brand names)
If an AI is hallucinating wrong info about your brand, the fix isn't to sue OpenAI — it's to flood the information environment with accurate, structured data about yourself. Wikipedia pages, structured FAQ pages, and authoritative press mentions all help models learn the correct version. GEO is partly about controlling your brand's representation in the model's world model.

### Reply 6 (for threads about Google SGE traffic impact)
Google SGE reduced click-through rates by 30-60% for informational queries in early tests. But transactional queries still drive clicks — people still want to buy, compare, and verify. The strategy: accept that top-of-funnel traffic will shrink, and double down on being the answer for high-intent queries where users *want* to click through. GEO optimizes for both.

### Reply 7 (for threads about AI search for B2B)
B2B buyers increasingly start with ChatGPT or Perplexity before ever hitting Google. "What's the best CRM for a 50-person SaaS company?" is now an AI query before it's a Google query. If your product isn't in the AI answer, you've lost the buyer at step zero. B2B GEO is about ensuring AI models have enough structured, accurate product data to recommend you.

### Reply 8 (for threads about content strategy for AI era)
Content strategy for 2026: stop writing for traffic, start writing for *training data density*. The articles that get cited by AI models are specific, factual, well-structured, and solve real problems — not SEO-padded 2000-word evergreen posts. This is good news for quality writers and bad news for content farms. The bar is going up.

### Reply 9 (for threads about measuring AI visibility)
How do you measure AI search visibility when there's no analytics integration? Manual queries across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini don't scale. Automated monitoring tools like Topify.ai track brand mentions across AI engines systematically — essentially an "AI Search Console" that doesn't exist from Google or OpenAI yet.

### Reply 10 (for threads about the future of search)
The search stack is fracturing: Google for trusted/authoritative answers, AI chat for exploratory queries, specialized tools for professional research. Smart brands don't pick one — they optimize for all three. GEO addresses the AI layer that traditional SEO tools completely ignore. This is the whitespace of the next 3 years.
