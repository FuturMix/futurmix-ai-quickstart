# 10 Small Businesses Thriving on X — May 2026

> A curated list of genuine small businesses and bootstrapped companies that are actively posting, engaging, and growing their audience on X (formerly Twitter). These are not Fortune 500 brands — they are lean teams, solo founders, and niche operators who have turned X into a real distribution channel.

---

## 1. TypingMind — @tdinh_me

- **Niche:** AI/Developer Tools (LLM chat frontend)
- **Approximate followers:** ~179K
- **Why they stand out:** Tony Dinh is the solo founder behind TypingMind, which generates $137K/month. He is one of the most prominent "build in public" practitioners on X, openly sharing revenue numbers, product decisions, and development updates — a masterclass in turning transparency into trust and sales.

---

## 2. Tally — @TallyForms

- **Niche:** SaaS / No-code Form Builder
- **Approximate followers:** ~14K
- **Why they stand out:** Founded by Marie Martens and bootstrapped to $4M ARR, Tally positions itself as a free Typeform alternative. Their X presence is clean and product-focused, regularly showcasing user-generated use cases and feature launches that keep their community engaged without a massive marketing budget.

---

## 3. Bannerbear — @bannerbearHQ

- **Niche:** SaaS / Automated Image & Video Generation API
- **Approximate followers:** ~6.2K
- **Why they stand out:** Jon Yongfook (@yongfook, ~154K personal followers) built Bannerbear as a solo founder to $50K+ MRR. The company account shares practical tutorials and integration ideas, while Jon's personal account is a textbook example of how a founder's personality can amplify a small product's reach by orders of magnitude.

---

## 4. Carrd — @carrd (founder: @ajlkn)

- **Niche:** Web Design / One-page Website Builder
- **Approximate followers:** ~65K (founder @ajlkn)
- **Why they stand out:** AJ built Carrd as a side project and bootstrapped it past $1M ARR, powering over 3.3 million websites with an essentially one-person team. The X account is minimal and product-driven — reflecting the tool's own philosophy of simplicity. AJ rarely does promotion, yet word-of-mouth from the X community keeps Carrd growing steadily.

---

## 5. Plausible Analytics — @PlausibleHQ

- **Niche:** Open Source / Privacy-first Web Analytics
- **Approximate followers:** ~18K
- **Why they stand out:** A self-funded, open-source alternative to Google Analytics that crossed $1M ARR without running paid ads, affiliate programs, or paying for endorsements. Their growth is almost entirely word-of-mouth, fueled by an X presence that emphasizes privacy advocacy and product transparency. Endorsed publicly by DHH (Basecamp/37signals).

---

## 6. ZenMaid — @ZenMaid (founder: @itsjustamar)

- **Niche:** Vertical SaaS / Cleaning Service Scheduling Software
- **Approximate followers:** ~3.8K (founder @itsjustamar)
- **Why they stand out:** ZenMaid is a niche SaaS serving maid service owners, built by Amar Ghose as a digital nomad to over $3M annual revenue. It is a perfect example of a hyper-targeted small business using X to engage a very specific professional community. Amar's thread celebrating crossing $100K MRR became a viral case study for bootstrapped SaaS founders.

---

## 7. Death Wish Coffee — @DeathWishCoffee

- **Niche:** D2C / Specialty Coffee Brand
- **Approximate followers:** ~100K+ (estimated; 343K on Instagram)
- **Why they stand out:** The self-proclaimed "world's strongest coffee" brand built its cult following through bold, irreverent social media content. They famously won a free Super Bowl ad through an Intuit contest, catapulting them from a tiny upstate New York coffee shop to a nationally recognized D2C brand. Their X voice is consistently witty, on-brand, and community-driven.

---

## 8. Beardbrand — @Beardbrand (founder: @bandholz)

- **Niche:** D2C / Men's Grooming & Beard Care
- **Approximate followers:** ~17K
- **Why they stand out:** Eric Bandholz built Beardbrand from a beard blog and YouTube channel into a multi-million-dollar grooming brand. On X, they blend product content with lifestyle storytelling and grooming tips, and are frequently cited as a case study in content-driven D2C growth. A small team that punches well above its weight in brand loyalty.

---

## 9. ShipFast / Marc Lou — @marc_louvion

- **Niche:** Indie SaaS / NextJS Boilerplate for Builders
- **Approximate followers:** ~127K
- **Why they stand out:** Marc Louvion built ShipFast (a NextJS boilerplate for indie hackers) to $60K+/month largely through his X presence. He is a prolific "build in public" creator who shares revenue screenshots, product experiments, and raw lessons learned. His unconventional, high-frequency posting style has become its own growth playbook, widely studied in the indie hacker community.

---

## 10. 4dayweek.io — @philostar

- **Niche:** Job Board / Future of Work
- **Approximate followers:** ~10K+ (estimated)
- **Why they stand out:** Phil McParlane, a former software engineer, quit his corporate job to build the world's largest job board dedicated to four-day work week positions. With 200K+ monthly users and 250+ companies listed, 4dayweek.io taps into one of the strongest workplace trends of 2026. Phil uses X to share data-driven posts about the four-day work week movement, attracting job seekers, employers, and media coverage alike.

---

## Key Takeaway

These businesses share a common thread: they treat X not as a broadcasting channel but as a conversation engine. Whether it is building in public (Tony Dinh, Marc Lou), championing a cause (Plausible's privacy stance, Phil's four-day week advocacy), or cultivating a niche community (ZenMaid's cleaning business owners, Beardbrand's grooming enthusiasts), the most effective small businesses on X in 2026 are the ones with a distinct voice and a genuine reason for their audience to care.
